Introduction




Rabindranath Tagore was born on 7 May, 1861. He was a great poet, philosopher, music composer and a leader of Brahma Samaj, who took the India culture and tradition to the whole world and became a voice of the Indian heritage.

Education

Tagore received his Education at home. He was taught in Bengali, with English lessons in the afternoon. In 1878, he went to England to become a barrister to fulfill his father's wish. He was not much interested in school learning. later he joined University College in London to learn law but he dropped this and learned various works of Shakespeare on his own.

Early life

He was born on 7 May, 1861 to Debendranath Tagore and Sarada Devi in the Jorasanko mansion which is the ancestral home of the Tagore family in Kolkata (Calcutta). Among his siblings, he was the youngest. Unfortunately, he lost his mother when he was very young. Since his father was a traveller, he was mostly raised by his servants and maids. At a very young age, he was part of the Bengal renaissance and his family also took active participation in it.

Works

  • His very first poem was published in Amrita Bazar Patrika at the age of 14.
  • Tagore is best known for his poetry. His poetry often explored themes of nature, love, spirituality, and human emotions.
  • One of his most famous works is the collection of poems titled "Gitanjali", which earned him the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913. .
  • He wrote approximately 2,232 songs. He composed his most famous and widely recognised song, 'Ekla Cholo Re'.

    • Anthems

    Tagore is the only person to have written anthems for three countries.

  • Jana Gana Mana, the national anthem of India.
  • Sri Lanka Matha, the national anthem of Sri Lanka.
  • Amar Shonar Bangla, the national anthem of Bangladesh.

    • Awards and Achievements

  • Tagore was honoured with the Nobel Prize in literature on 14 Nov, 1913.
  • He was also conferred knighthood in 1915, which he renounced in 1919 after the Jallianwala Bagh carnage.
  • Establishment of Shantiniketan - In 1902, Rabindranath Tagore relocated his father's purchased land to establish an 'ashram' and open an experimental school.

    • Death

    He passed away on 7 August, 1941, at the age of 80. He was diagnosed with severe uraemia and a blocked urinary bladder.
